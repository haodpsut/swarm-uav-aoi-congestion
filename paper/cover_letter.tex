We are pleased to submit our manuscript entitled ``Congestion-Aware Joint
Charging-Station Placement, Capacity, and Swarm-UAV Trajectory Design for
Age-Optimal Data Collection'' for consideration for publication in the
\textit{IEEE Internet of Things Journal}.

Unmanned aerial vehicles are increasingly used to collect time-sensitive data
from Internet of Things sensors, where the relevant quality metric is the Age of
Information. Practical deployments use a swarm of energy-limited UAVs that must
recharge at a small number of shared charging stations. This creates a problem
that a single UAV never faces: the charging stations become a contended resource,
and the resulting waiting time inflates the Age of Information. Deciding where to
place the stations, how many charging ports to install, how the swarm should fly,
and which UAV recharges where is a coupled design problem that, to our knowledge,
has not been addressed as a whole.

The manuscript makes the following contributions:

\begin{itemize}
\item We formulate the first joint charging-station placement, port-capacity,
  swarm-trajectory, and assignment problem for peak-Age-of-Information data
  collection with a contended, multi-server charging resource.
\item We show that the charging system must be modeled as a finite-source
  (machine-repair) queue rather than an open $M/M/c$ queue, and we validate this
  against a discrete-event simulation: the open queue overpredicts the waiting
  time by roughly six times and would distort the design.
\item We prove two structural results: a congestion threshold that makes the Age
  of Information non-monotone in the swarm size, with a provisioning law linking
  the optimal swarm size to the charging capacity, and a placement inversion in
  which the optimal placement becomes traffic-driven rather than coverage-driven.
\item We design a block-coordinate solver with a GPU close-enough
  traveling-salesman trajectory optimizer, and we evaluate it against
  single-UAV, single-station, coverage-optimal, and round-robin baselines,
  including a large-swarm scaling study, a robustness study, and an
  optimality-gap study against the exact optimum.
\end{itemize}

We believe the topic is well aligned with the scope of the
\textit{IEEE Internet of Things Journal} and will interest its readership working
on UAV-assisted data collection, Age of Information, and energy-aware Internet of
Things systems.

We confirm that this manuscript is original, has not been published previously,
